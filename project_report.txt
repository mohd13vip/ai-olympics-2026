# 🪞 Game 1 — The Mirror Maze
## Can You Trust the Score?

Your team has received the first version of the competition dataset.

A quick baseline gives a promising validation score. At first glance, the model seems to understand the difference between Real and Fake content.

But in real AI projects, a good score is not always a good sign.

Sometimes the validation set reflects true generalization.  
Sometimes it reflects a hidden problem in the data preparation process.

Your mission is to investigate the dataset before improving the model.

Do not start by building a larger model.

Start by asking:

> Is this validation score trustworthy?

You are given image-text samples, labels, and a baseline result.  
Your task is to discover whether there is any hidden issue that makes the evaluation unreliable, prove it with evidence, fix the dataset split, and compare the result before and after your correction.

# **Game 1 Requirements**
- - -
## 🎯 Game Objective

Investigate the provided training and validation data and determine whether the initial validation score can be trusted.

Your team must:

1. Run the provided baseline.
2. Analyze the training and validation samples.
3. Search for hidden relationships between the two splits.
4. Provide evidence for any discovered issue.
